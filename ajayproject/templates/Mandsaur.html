About Mandsaur

Mandsaur is a culturally significant city in Madhya Pradesh, known for its historic temples, sculptures, and archaeological heritage. It lies on the border of Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh and is home to the famous Pashupatinath Temple with its unique eight-faced Shiva idol.

Top Attractions

  • Pashupatinath Temple: A major pilgrimage site with an eight-faced Shiva Linga.
  • Gandhi Sagar Sanctuary: A scenic wildlife sanctuary on the banks of the Chambal River.
  • Dashpur Fort: Ruins of an ancient fort telling tales of medieval history.
  • Bandi Ji Ka Bagh: A beautiful Jain temple complex and garden area.
  • Mandsaur Museum: Explore rare sculptures and archaeological artifacts.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Mandsaur is between October and February, when the climate is cool and perfect for sightseeing and temple visits.

How to Reach

By Air: Nearest airport is Indore Airport (approx. 210 km).
By Train: Mandsaur Railway Station is well connected to Ujjain, Ratlam, and Kota.
By Road: Good road connectivity via National Highway 156 and 113.
